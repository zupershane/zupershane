Airbnb week of good

Concept design & direction

Brief

A micro-library

Over a 24 hour period, design a micro-library for children of the Syrian refugee camp in Zaatari. The microlibrary had to be easily manufactured, transported, durable, easily assembled, and aesthetically unique. It also needed to be mobile.

Role

Product designer

I was responsible for the concept, delivery and prototype throughout the design sprint. Partnering with other designers and researchers, we looked at various designs and materials, to finally land on a flat-pack style approach.

Process

Conceive, iterate, prototype

In a small team of designers and researchers, we set out to conceive, iterate, and prototype a working model. The design was chosen by a panel of architects and humanitarian aid members as a finalist in the Airbnb Week of Good design challenge.

Zaatari refugee camp

Zaatari is a refugee camp in Jordan. It was first opened on July 28, 2012 to host Syrians fleeing the violence in the ongoing Syrian civil war that erupted in 2011. On March 26, 2015, the camp population was estimated at 83,000 refugees.
