SBS World News

Original Product design & future vision

First of its kind

Appstore hall of fame

SBS World News launch as one of the first native universal apps in Australia designed for both iPhone and iPad. Shortly after it’s launch it quickly became the number one news app in Australia and was inducted into the Apple App Store Hall of Fame where it remained for 3 years.

The current live version doesn’t quite reflect the original product design and vision as SBS have departed from the native (and somewhat bespoke) design of the past. After looking back on the designs, with its overt skeuomorphic overtones, I really wanted to revisit the product to see how the original ideas would translate using modern design patterns like iOS 8.

Design Journal
Figure 1.1 Original homepage

Skeuomorphic overtones, gradients and patterns were all the rage in 2010. It's funny how design patterns and trends change so quickly, especially when iOS 7 was introduced.

Figure 1.2 Font-scaling

One of the goals with the original design was to enhance the reading experience on a mobile by using traditional typographic principles and giving readers additional tools such as in-situ font scaling.

Figure 1.3 Multi-media controls

SBS offered more content to their readers in the form of photo galleries, videos and podcasts. 'Power-user' controls were embedded in each article to create a more immersive reading experience.
